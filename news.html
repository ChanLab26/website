Grant
K23 Application Under Review at NIMH Study Section
Andy's K23 Mentored Patient-Oriented Research Career Development Award, "Characterizing Frontal-Limbic Circuit Specific Subtype of Depression in Epilepsy Using Intracranial EEG" (Award No. KMH147414A), has been accepted for review at the July 2025 ZRG1 SEP study section at NIMH. This is the primary funding vehicle for the lab's biomarker discovery program.

2025
Publication
SeizEAR Manuscript Submitted to Sensors (MDPI)
Our in-ear EEG platform manuscript has been submitted to Sensors (MDPI), describing a novel Pearson r²-derived SNR metric for ambulatory interictal epileptiform discharge detection using the external auditory canal as a recording site. The manuscript was substantially co-written by medical student Ryan. Developed in collaboration with the Achin Bhowmik lab at Starkey Hearing Technologies.

Grant
SENTINEL RAG Proposal Submitted — Epilepsy & Sleep Comorbidity in Appalachia
Andy co-investigated a Research Activity Grant (RAG) proposal, "SENTINEL," led by Dr. Lulu Guo (WVU Neurology) targeting care coordination for patients with comorbid epilepsy and sleep disorders in rural Appalachian communities. Proposal focuses on telehealth-enabled, community health worker-mediated outreach.

Presentation
Grand Rounds: "Epilepsy Beyond the Brain" — WVU Internal Medicine
Andy delivered an invited grand rounds to WVU Internal Medicine addressing the systemic interactions of seizures, antiseizure medications, and surgical management, including cardiac, metabolic, endocrine, and psychiatric comorbidities in epilepsy. Designed to improve recognition and co-management of epilepsy among hospitalists and internists.

Grand Rounds: "Epilepsy as a Brain Disease Across the Lifespan" — WVU Family Medicine
Andy co-presented a grand rounds lecture to WVU Family Medicine with Dr. D. Kojo Adelson (Pediatric Neurology), covering the developmental epidemiology of epilepsy, seizure recognition, first-line treatment, and indications for subspecialty referral across pediatric and adult populations.

2025
Presentation
Letter of Intent Submitted — Focused Ultrasound Foundation SEEG Biomarker Study
Andy submitted a Letter of Intent to the Focused Ultrasound Foundation for a mechanistic SEEG-based study of low-intensity focused ultrasound (LIFU) effects on human intracranial oscillatory activity. Proposes ANT-targeted LIFU using NaviFUS technology, with real-time stereoEEG biomarker acquisition as the primary outcome.

Grant
K23 Application Submitted to NIMH
Andy submitted the K23 Mentored Patient-Oriented Research Career Development Award application to NIMH after an 18-month development process. NSF IUCRC BRAIN Pilot Subaward — AR-BCI Aphasia Study

Lab Member
Andy Joins WVU Rockefeller Neuroscience Institute as Assistant Professor
Ho Wing (Andy) Chan, MD officially joined the WVU Department of Neurology as Assistant Professor and Epileptologist-Physician Scientist. He established the Chan Lab (Neural Circuits and Neurotechnology Laboratory) at the Rockefeller Neuroscience Institute, bringing an intracranial electrophysiology and neuromodulation research program focused on epilepsy-depression comorbidity, BCI development, and digital biomarkers.
